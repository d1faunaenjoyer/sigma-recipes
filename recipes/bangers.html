Bangers and Mash

A British "pub classic". Bangers and Mash is a staple in every pub known to man consisting of pork sausages (the bangers), mashed potatoes, peas, and onion gravy.

This recipe is simple and simultaneously good. Let's keep it that way with this recipe.

Notes:

  • If you are Muslim, you can substitute the pork sausage for a Halal meat of your own choice
  • The sausage part of this recipe cannot be avoided. Please use a sausage of some sort.
  • Avoid using lean sausages. If you need extra fat, use lard or beef tallow.
  • I heavily recommend that you buy fresh peas for this dish.

Ingredients:

  • One and a half onions.
  • Three cloves of garlic.
  • A quarter-cup of plain flour.
  • Black pepper to taste.
  • 1-2 cups of beef stock.

Cooking:

  • Brown the sausages on a pan. Remove them once they are brown on both sides. Feel free to remove a little bit of fat but make sure there is enough to cook the onion and garlic.
  • After browning the sausages, sautee the onion and garlic until they are soft.
  • Let them caramellise slightly before introducing the flour into the pan. Cook off the flour with a knob of butter to form a roux. This increases the richness of the gravy. Keep stirring until the roux is smooth and not lumpy.
  • Then, pour the beef stock into the roux. You do not have to use all of it. Ensure that the gravy is not as consistent as water.
  • I would teach you how to make mashed potatoes, but that is pretty self-explanatory. Everyone has their own recipe, so use your favourite.
  • Finally, plate up nicely and enjoy!
